### It's the last day of the year.

Welp, another year has passed. I'm just going to dump as much of what I can 
remember of it here, grouped by month. Let's see how many projects I started
(and how many I finished, which might turn out to be less than I thought.)

This is going to be super rambly.

### January

In January, I was just getting back into school. I got super obsessed with 
[Nathan Barnatt](https://www.youtube.com/c/barnatt)'s dance videos for some 
reason, and that got me into Madeon and Yelle's music. 

I learned how to write C++. It wasn't very good C++, but it was C++ nonetheless, 
and I tried making a game engine with it, which was riddled with memory leaks
and unsafe code. I ended up abandoning the project within a week or two. Why
is it that I abandon projects after about a week? Is it because it's that 
awkward period between seeing that I've made a minimum viable product and
having made a finished, polished project? I think so. Oh well.

### February

I made my school's varsity volleyball team in February. I was so happy because
it meant that my quitting of club volleyball didn't make me worse at the sport.
(It didn't make me better either, but at least I didn't get worse.) During this
time, I had less and less time to do programming projects due to school starting
up again, AP tests looming in the next three months, and volleyball games ending
around nine at night.

In this month, I tried out making a little visual program called [ember](https://github.com/liu7d7/ember) 
whose main premise was that it was post-processed with an eight-color palette. 
It looked super cool, and it's one of the projects I'm most proud of to this day.

Another thing I did was learn React, which I used to make a [website](https://liu7d7.github.io/french-subj/) 
to help me review some French.

I also messed around with using C to make some data structures from scratch, which
was a fun exercise, and it made me able to program some larger projects in C later on.
You can find the repo [here](https://github.com/liu7d7/structs-in-c), though I'm 
not very proud of the implementations there.

### March

I got COVID during March, which gave me a good two weeks to do
whatever the heck I wanted, as long as it was in my room. Now, this wasn't any
different to what I was already doing, but at least I didn't have to go to school.

I started up coding some of my old projects again, and extended my C# engine to
support deformable meshes. I renamed the project [new](https://github.com/liu7d7/new),
because I couldn't be arsed to come up with a new name.

### April

During April, I was cramming as hard as I could for AP tests, which were coming
up in just a month, meaning I couldn't program as much. I did have one significant
project though, which was a 1000-liner game of Tennis for Two programmed in F#
using OpenGL for graphics. The game was for the free-response assignment to the
AP Computer Science Principles test, which was actually really fun to make.

During the limited free-time I had, I continued working on that game engine, adding
support for instanced rendering (using UBOs, though, which is not ideal). The 
amount of trees and snowballs I could render was never higher!

### May

I feel like I had a local maxima in May, honestly. I had much more time to do
projects, because AP tests were over within the first week of the month, and I
knew I could breeze through the school finals. Making things better for my
programming career, the volleyball season also ended, which gave me a lot more
free time. I started up running, and though I wasn't very good, I kept at it. 

During this month, I was applying to become an officer in my school's CS club,
and one of the main things I did was work on a guest lecture for them. The
presentation was about OpenGL, and to give some extra flair to it, I made the
whole [presentation](https://github.com/liu7d7/lecture) _in_ OpenGL. That was 
very fun indeed, and I think it was received well. I made a different guest 
lecture presentation for a [fractal lecture](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N4bHzI8Z0ik)
for the math club as well.

### June

School's out! Yay! Let's see what I did in June. I got back into C++, and made a
little demo program that could do skeletal animation, which was really cool, but
I didn't want to continue that project. I also started up trying to make different
programming languages, and I had help from one of my friends, Aadi Yadav.

I also made it as a CS club officer, and started a course on digital circuits and
boolean logic at UCLA, which was actually really fun.

### July

The main thing I tried out in July was doing some freelance work on UpWork. I
actually got a job offer really quick, which was nice. The project was making a
ring fit around the ring finger of a video of a hand, using OpenCV to find joints
and OpenGL to render the ring. 

I learned a lot about linear algebra and how matrices work with this project,
and it was fascinating to do, and I had the potential of earning $240 for
the job.

Sadly, I never read the terms and conditions before taking the job, so I never
found out that you had to be over 18 to get paid. How unfortunate.

### August

This month, I took a brilliant trip to China with my family. We climbed mountains, 
ran in parks, and ate some Chinese BBQ. I also developed an addiction to
eggplants, which I will now ramble about. The restaurant fried them or something,
which turned out brilliant because the skin and open faces of the inside of the
eggplant were crispy and the middle was soft and fluffy. The sweet and sour sauce
was so good too, and it really made the dish. Peppercorns and some sort of green herb added that
little extra. I think I've dedicated enough of this to eggplants now, though.

Another thing I did was make [koneko](https://github.com/liu7d7/koneko), a little BASIC interpreter that had easy-to-use
line and polygon fill graphics commands, and ran in Rust. It was a really fun
project to make and I sometimes still use it to make some simple visual stuff
or toy around with cool syntax. You can see a demo of it [here](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vXzDkM7uB44).

[![new](http://img.youtube.com/vi/vXzDkM7uB44/0.jpg)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vXzDkM7uB44)

### September

School started up again, and I had to endure my school's infamous Honors Chemistry
teacher. I failed my first test, with a score of about 53%. Goddamn it. That set
me up for a semester of self-loathing and stress. And no programming to give me
a break, either. My social life suffered and I think I had the most anxiety 
(social anxiety included) during this time too.

### October

My grade wasn't suffering as much this month, so I got to work on a [game](https://github.com/liu7d7/hana)
engine in Rust. I got to learn that Rust is actually a brilliant language and 
it's just so much nicer compared to C++. You don't have to worry about memory
leaks or going out of bounds or anything, and it's just such a joy to write
when you're not fighting with the borrow checker. I'd rather fight with said
borrow checker than try to track down where a nullptr sneaked in or where I'm
leaking memory, though.

### November

Ah, AMC 10 month. I failed. That's all I want to say.

I also went on a day trip to San Francisco for me and my friend's birthday, 
which was absolutely fantastic. We did a sort of thing where we raced to do a 
set of specific tasks, and though my team lost, we had so much fun. From eating
a whole raw serrano and throwing up to taking group pictures

### December

This is the section that'll have the most mustard on it. I feel like this month
was the peak of my 2023. For physical education, I joined a dance group with some
friends and did k-pop, which was really enjoyable. It helped me gain some new 
friends and learn how to dance at the same time, and was some much-needed social
time during finals week.

Speaking of finals, I actually didn't fail them. Though I was stressed because 
I didn't study as much as I probably should have for some of them, the went fine,
and I did surprisingly well on the chemistry final. 96%. That brought my grade
heavily into the A category, which made me happy but not that happy.

For programming, I went back to C# for a game engine project called 
[for-realsies](https://github.com/liu7d7/for-realsies) (you can tell I'm frustrated 
with giving up), and this time I integrated the Bepu Physics engine, which is 
actually really nice to work with, with a low barrier to entry and super nice 
examples. I integrated that dithering code I used for [ember](https://github.com/liu7d7/ember/), 
and it looks really nice!
